About Me

I'm a PhD candidate in Plant Science at the University of Connecticut, specializing in remote sensing of crop stress. My research focuses on integrating drone-based technologies to detect and quantify stress factors affecting crop health, specifically studying Potato leafhopper infestation and nitrogen deficiency in beans using multispectral and hyperspectral imaging.

Research

My work integrates advanced remote sensing technologies, including drone-based multispectral imaging and hyperspectral sensors, to improve precision agriculture practices. I am passionate about developing innovative solutions to enhance crop health and productivity.

  • Detection of Potato Leafhopper stress in beans using drone imagery.
  • Hyperspectral sensing of crop nutrient deficiencies.
  • Risk assessment of invasive insects in apple orchards.

Research Lab

My research takes place at the UConn Plant Science Research and Education Facility.

I also collaborate with the Advanced Remote Sensing Imaging & Analytics Lab (ARIAL) at UConn.

Feel free to reach out for research collaborations, presentations, or discussions about remote sensing applications in agriculture.
